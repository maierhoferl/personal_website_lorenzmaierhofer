Five Ways to Train

Jaw Muscles

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything parents, therapists, and educators want to know about Grimasso.

Myofunctional therapy is a program of exercises that retrain the muscles of the tongue, lips, and face. It helps correct tongue thrust, mouth breathing, and improper swallowing patterns — issues that can affect speech, dental development, and airway health.

Grimasso is designed around evidence-based myofunctional therapy principles used by speech-language pathologists (SLPs). Many SLPs use it as a homework compliance tool for between-session practice. Learn more on our For Professionals page.

Grimasso's tongue strengthening and mobility exercises are often recommended as part of pre- and post-frenectomy myofunctional therapy programs. Always follow your SLP's or surgeon's specific guidance for your child's situation.

Yes. Every exercise in Grimasso is based on established myofunctional therapy protocols — targeting tongue tip placement, tongue strength, swallowing patterns, jaw mobility, and lip function. The gamification layer is designed to maximize compliance and consistency, which is the #1 predictor of therapy outcomes.

Yes! Grimasso has a PIN-protected parent dashboard where you can monitor your child's progress, see which exercises were completed, manage multiple child profiles, and set daily screen time limits.

Grimasso is designed for children ages 7–12, though younger children can use it with parental guidance and older children and teens benefit just as much. The animated frog guide and gamification make it engaging across a wide age range.

Grimasso is completely free to download and use. There are no in-app purchases, no subscription fees, no ads, and no third-party data collection from children. All 100 exercises and 20 levels are available from day one.
